How to Google

The Art of Looking Stuff Up

There was a time when people simply didn't know. Actually learning the factual answer to a question took work. It involved owning things like expensive encyclopedia sets, or going to a library and hoping that they had relevant and current books on the topic you wanted to research. It was tough, back in 1997.

In 2018, however, the whole of human knowledge is at your fingertips. All you have to do is look it up.

Here's how it works: Click this link and you'll open a new tab that contains a Google search bar. Now, think about your question. Let's say I'm wondering if the Earth is flat. That would be a good thing to know.

Just type it in. 'Is the Earth flat?' When I try it, I see this: 'About 328,000,000 results (0.31 seconds).' The vast majority of them say it is not. Now I know.

How many results do you get? How different would the world be if people used this simple technique?

It probably wouldn't even be flat, anymore.
